# Residual Correction Dataset Sources

Date: 2026-03-31

Goal: find measured pigment-mixture data that can be transformed into binary
training examples of the form `color A + color B = color C` for residual
correction experiments.

## Selection Criteria

- Real physical measurements, not synthetic blends generated by another library.
- Known mixture composition or ratios.
- Enough spectral or color data to recover both the inputs and the measured mixed
  target.
- Prefer public downloads over request-only access.
- Prefer paint media that are reasonably close to our target use case.

## Best Candidates

### 1. Sensors 2021: oil-paint mixture mockups

Source article:
- https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/21/7/2471

Why it is promising:
- Seven real Kremer pigments mixed in linseed oil on canvas.
- 175 measured mixture patches covering mixtures with up to three pigments.
- Binary mixtures were prepared for all pigment pairs.
- VNIR hyperspectral capture from 400-1000 nm, trimmed to 166 spectral points.
- Data availability statement says the study data are in the supplementary
  material.

Why it fits `A + B = C`:
- We can start by keeping only the binary mixtures.
- Pure-pigment swatches act as the measured `A` and `B` endpoints.
- The measured binary patch becomes `C`.

Main caveats:
- The article page is clear that the data are in the supplement, but the exact
  file structure needs inspection before scripting ingestion.
- Ratios appear to be a small discrete set rather than a dense sweep.

Initial recommendation:
- Best first dataset to operationalize because it is physically measured, medium
  matched to oil paint, and should be lighter-weight than the larger heritage
  libraries.

### 2. Analytical and Bioanalytical Chemistry 2025: manuscript pigment and dye mockups

Source article:
- https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00216-025-05948-3

Public dataset:
- https://doi.org/10.6084/m9.figshare.28639103.v1

Why it is promising:
- 156 physically prepared mock-ups.
- Includes 39 biphasic mixtures, 34 triphasic mixtures, and 2 quadriphasic
  mixtures.
- Exact composition is described in the database general information.
- Publicly released on Figshare.
- Includes 400 reflectance spectra per mock-up.

Why it fits `A + B = C`:
- The cleanest slice is the biphasic and triphasic subset representing two
  colorants plus binder.
- Uniform mixtures can be kept; superimposed layers can be excluded.

Main caveats:
- Medium mismatch: manuscript materials, gum Arabic, egg glair, paper, and
  parchment are not the same as oil-paint mixing.
- The set includes dyes as well as pigments, so filtering is required.
- Binder/support variation may need to be treated as metadata or a nuisance
  factor.

Initial recommendation:
- Strong second dataset because it is public, large, and carefully documented,
  but less medium-aligned than the oil-paint datasets.

### 3. Zenodo 2024: VNIR and SWIR HSI library of unvarnished oil paints on canvas

Dataset:
- https://zenodo.org/records/13359559

Why it is promising:
- Real oil-paint libraries built on canvas.
- Includes oil colours and mixture swatches.
- Provides `.xlsx` spectral files plus composition/layout documentation in a
  companion PDF.
- Includes both handmade and tube-paint libraries.

Why it fits `A + B = C`:
- Mixed swatches can be paired with their corresponding endmember swatches using
  the composition PDF and the spreadsheet spectra.
- The medium is much closer to our target than manuscript datasets.

Main caveats:
- The full dataset is large.
- Composition extraction may require parsing layout metadata from the PDF.
- Licensing is CC BY-NC-ND, so we should check downstream redistribution
  assumptions before repackaging derived files.

Initial recommendation:
- Very good candidate once we are ready to invest a bit more ingestion effort.

### 4. npj Heritage Science 2025: Thangka mineral pigments with binary mixtures

Source article:
- https://www.nature.com/articles/s40494-025-02241-5

Why it is promising:
- 12 pure pigments plus 54 binary mixtures.
- Six pigment pairs measured at nine weight ratios each.
- 170 representative spectra per sample.
- Reflectance acquisition in the 400-1000 nm range.

Why it fits `A + B = C`:
- This is almost a direct binary-mixture dataset once the spectra are obtained.
- Weight-ratio sweeps are useful for testing whether residual correction improves
  monotonicity and interpolation.

Main caveats:
- Data availability is by request to the corresponding author, not direct
  download.
- The palette is narrow and focused on mineral pigments used in Thangka
  paintings.
- Binder and substrate differ from our likely runtime use cases.

Initial recommendation:
- Excellent request-only backup or validation set, especially for binary-only
  experiments.

### 5. Talanta 2016 / University of Bath dataset: binary paint mixtures with public spectra

Source article:
- https://research.manchester.ac.uk/en/publications/estimation-of-semiconductor-like-pigment-concentrations-in-paint-

Public dataset record:
- https://researchportal.bath.ac.uk/en/datasets/data-for-estimation-of-semiconductor-like-pigment-concentrations-/

Dataset DOI:
- https://doi.org/10.15125/BATH-00183

Why it is promising:
- The Bath dataset record explicitly says it contains a complete set of
  reflectance spectra recorded from the paint samples prepared for the study.
- The underlying study focuses on binary paint mixtures and distinguishes
  mixtures from layers.
- Real measured paint samples, not synthetic mixtures generated by a library.

Why it fits `A + B = C`:
- The paper studies binary mixtures of three pigments:
  vermilion, red lead, and lead-tin yellow.
- In principle, pure samples plus binary mixtures should map directly into
  measured `A + B = C` rows.

Main caveats:
- The palette is very narrow and skewed toward warm historical pigments.
- We still need to inspect the downloadable files to confirm sample naming,
  ratio coverage, and whether the composition metadata is explicit enough for
  automated ingestion.
- It may include both mixtures and layered samples, so filtering could be
  required.

Initial recommendation:
- Best next concrete public lead after the current four sources because the data
  record itself claims complete reflectance spectra for the prepared paint
  samples.

## Lower-Confidence Follow-Ups

### A. Heritage Science 2020: mixed pigments for Chinese paintings

Source article:
- https://www.nature.com/articles/s40494-020-00372-5

Why it may be worth a look:
- Lab-prepared mixed pigments for Chinese paintings.
- Focuses on identifying pigment type and proportion in mixtures.

Main caveat:
- I have not found a linked public dataset so far, and it may only provide
  figures plus method description rather than reusable spectra tables.

### B. npj Heritage Science 2018: Van Gogh reconstructed palette

Source article:
- https://www.nature.com/articles/s40494-018-0181-6

Why it may be worth a look:
- Physically reconstructed oil paints and optical characterization.
- Strong medium relevance for oil-paint behavior.

Main caveat:
- This looks more useful for optical-parameter or base-model work than for a
  clean measured binary-mixture dataset, and I have not yet found a public raw
  spectra release.

## Sources To Avoid As Primary Residual-Correction Data

- Pure-pigment spectral libraries without measured mixtures.
- Datasets dominated by superimposed layers rather than physically mixed paints.
- Datasets lacking explicit composition metadata.

These can still help with base-model calibration or endmember lookup, but they
do not directly solve the `A + B = C` supervision problem.

## Suggested Next Step

Start with the `Sensors 2021` dataset first, then use the `Figshare 2025`
mock-up database as a scale-up/robustness set. That gives us:

- one medium-aligned oil-paint source,
- one larger public source with richer nuisance variation,
- and a clean path to compare residual correction across homogeneous vs.
  heterogeneous acquisition conditions.
